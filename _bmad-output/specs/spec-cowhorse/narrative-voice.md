# Narrative Voice — CowHorse

## Multi-voice narrative mode

CowHorse uses an "E" mode multi-voice mix. Different narrative voices are rendered with distinct visual treatments so the player can feel the shift without explicit labels.

| Voice | Role | Visual treatment |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Inner monologue | The protagonist's unfiltered thoughts | Italic, slightly muted color, slower typewriter speed |
| Social voice | External expectations (family, boss, society) | Bold, clipped phrases, boxed callouts |
| Meme voice | Internet culture commentary | Sticker-style badges, larger emoji, collage cut-out frames |
| Quiet voice | Loneliness moments and emotional anchors | Minimal UI, full-screen text, no choice panel |

## Emotional tone

The default register is satirical and meme-rich. Heavy or quiet moments are deliberate punctuation, not decoration. A heavy moment should land because the surrounding tone has been funny.

- Use humor to lower the player's guard; use quiet moments to make the trade-off real.
- Avoid preachy conclusions. Show the cost of a choice through attribute effects and post-choice narration, not explicit moralizing.
- The game does not judge the player for choosing a "bad person" route; it shows consequences through endings and Life Resume entries.

## Meme and reference boundaries

Memes are game mechanics (CAP-8) and social texture. They must stay recognizable to the target audience and avoid narrow references that will age in six months.

**Stable categories:**
- Workplace: 996, OKR, 复盘, 优化毕业, 向上管理
- Job search: 学历贬值, 海投, 群面, 已读不回, offer 被鸽
- Exams: 二战三战, 考公热, 调剂
- Family pressure: 催婚, 催考公, 别人家孩子
- Internet slang: 鼠鼠我呀, 孔乙己长衫, 躺平, 摆烂, 润
- Body/mind: 脱发, 失眠, 体检异常, 出租屋孤独想哭

**Usage rule:** A meme must be contextually correct to reward the player. Forced memes in serious moments backfire (debuff).

## Content principles

- Fiction first: events are exaggerated but feel "懂的都懂."
- Numbers lean real where possible (rent, salary ranges, exam dates) to anchor the satire.
- No content is off-limits as long as it serves the tone and does not break the fictional frame.
